Other Projects

The images below show my past projects from various class assignments. Each demonstrates a different skill set that I developed through these assignments and the work ethic that I obtained from these projects to apply to future projects.

This Thin Veil

In this piece, I strived to represent a physical space in a 2D graphic. Through trial and error, I learned the importance of type to inform the viewer of a concept that is not inherent in the piece and how to catalog a collection of distinct images to a product that leads to a single idea.

Color Wheel

This piece was my first time working with acrylic paint. This work taught me the function of color and the crucial role of layering. I learned how hues can be used strategically in an illustration and the advantages of vibrant colors when contrasted to duller ones.

Realism

My design studies started with focusing on rendering realism. This piece demonstrates attention to detail to shadows and proportions. From this piece, I learned the importance of an accurate representation of elements since even the slightest alteration could result in an unrealistic product.
